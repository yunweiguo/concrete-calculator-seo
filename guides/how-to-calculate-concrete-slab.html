How to Calculate Concrete for a Slab (e.g., Patio or Foundation)

Calculating the amount of concrete for a rectangular slab is a common task for many DIY and professional projects. Getting it right prevents waste and ensures you have enough material to complete the job. This guide will walk you through it.

Step 1: Measure the Dimensions

First, you need three measurements for your slab. For consistency, it's best to take these measurements in the same unit (e.g., all in feet or all in meters).

  • Length: The longest side of the slab.
  • Width: The shorter side of the slab.
  • Thickness (or Height/Depth): How thick the slab will be. This is often measured in inches but needs to be converted to feet for the calculation.

Important: To convert inches to feet, divide the number of inches by 12. For example, a 6-inch thick slab is 6 / 12 = 0.5 feet.

Step 2: Calculate the Volume

The formula for the volume of a rectangular slab is straightforward:

Volume = Length × Width × Thickness

When you multiply these three numbers (in feet), the result will be in cubic feet (ft³).

Example Calculation:

Let's say you're building a patio with the following dimensions:

  • Length = 12 feet
  • Width = 10 feet
  • Thickness = 4 inches (which is 4 / 12 = 0.333 feet)

Using the formula:

Volume = 12 ft × 10 ft × 0.333 ft = 40 cubic feet

Step 3: Convert to Cubic Yards (Optional but Recommended)

Concrete is most often ordered and sold in cubic yards (yd³). To convert your result from cubic feet to cubic yards, you need to divide by 27 (since there are 27 cubic feet in 1 cubic yard).

Volume in Cubic Yards = Volume in Cubic Feet / 27

Example Continued:

Taking our previous result of 40 cubic feet:

Volume = 40 ft³ / 27 = 1.48 cubic yards

So, you would need to order approximately 1.5 cubic yards of concrete.
